### Extra
The most gravity-dependent site of the peritoneal cavity in the supine position is the hepatorenal space (Morrison's pouch). Gastric content released into the peritoneal cavity from a perforated peptic ulcer tends to collect in this space and can become infected, resulting in a subhepatic abscess.
