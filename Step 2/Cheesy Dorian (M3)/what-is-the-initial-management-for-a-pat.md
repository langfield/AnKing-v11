### Text
What is the <i>initial management</i> for a patient with
<u>pulmonary hypertension</u> that presents with <b>JVD</b>,
<b>peripheral edema</b>, <b>bibasilar crackles</b>, and an
<b>ejection fraction of 30%</b>?
<div>
  {{c1::Loop diuretics and ACE inhibitors (or ARBs)}}
</div>

### Extra
<i>the pulmonary hypertension is due to <b>LV systolic
dysfunction</b> and thus treatment involves addressing the
underlying cause; diuretics will help with fluid overload and
ACE-inhibitors will prevent further cardiac remodeling;</i>
<b><i>Î²</i></b><i><b>-blockers</b> and occasionally
<b>aldosterone</b> <b>antagonists</b> may be used for long-term
management as well (note: endothelin-1 receptor antagonists and
PDE-5 inhibitors are used for <u>idiopathic</u> pulmonary
hypertension)</i>
