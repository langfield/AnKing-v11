### Text
Why is an <u>abdominal ultrasound</u> <i>not</i> the best initial
imaging modality for patients with suspected <b>chronic
pancreatitis</b>?
<div>
  {{c1::Pancreas is a retroperitoneal organ that is not easily
  visualized through the overlying bowel loops}}
</div>

### Extra
An abdominal ultrasound can be used to detect <u>late findings</u>
of chronic pancreatitis including <u>pancreatic ductal
calcifications</u> and/or ductal irregularity/dilation, which are
pathognomonic features of chronic pancreatitis, as well as
pancreatic pseudocysts, a common complication.
