### Text
What is the likely <i>diagnosis</i> in an elderly patient that
presents with worsening <b>severe</b> <b>left-sided ear pain</b>
with an <font color="#FC0280">edematous</font> auditory canal and
<u>purulent discharge and <b>granulation</b> tissue</u>visible on
otoscopic exam?
<div>
  {{c1::Malignant (necrotizing) otitis externa}}
</div>

### Extra
<i>usually caused by <b>Pseudomonas aeruginosa</b>; presence of
<u>granulation</u> tissue, elevated <u>ESR</u>, and <u>severe</u>
pain distinguish MOE from typical otitis externa; <b>facial
droop</b> may be present due to spread to CN7; MOE is an
<b>osteomyelitis</b> of the external auditory canal spreading to
the skull base.</i>
