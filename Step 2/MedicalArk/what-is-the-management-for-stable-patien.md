### Text
What is the <i>management</i> for stable patients with <b>acute
mesenteric ischemia</b> after <u>medical therapy</u>?
<div>
  {{c1::Balloon angioplasty and stenting}}
</div>

### Extra
Performing balloon angioplasty and stenting is the preferred
treatment for hemodynamically stable patients with acute mesenteric
ischemia who do not present with signs or symptoms of advanced
intestinal ischemia (peritonitis, sepsis) because this procedure is
minimally invasive and studies suggest similar efficacy to open
surgical treatment
<div>
  Explorative laparotomy is reserved for acutely ill patients who
  are at risk for rapid deterioration, including patients with
  acute mesenteric ischemia who are hemodynamically unstable
</div>
