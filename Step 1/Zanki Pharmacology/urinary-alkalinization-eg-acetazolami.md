### Text
<div>
  <b>Urinary alkalinization</b> (e.g. acetazolamide) can
  <u>increase</u> <i>solubility</i> of <b>{{c1::cystine}}</b> and
  <b>{{c2::uric acid}}</b>kidney stones
</div>

### Extra
Alkalinization of urine traps acidic species (making them ionized)
<div>
  UWORLD: Some studies have shown that acetazolamide may raise the
  risk of calcium oxalate stones (acetazolamide causes metabolic
  acidosis which may cause release of CaPO<sub>4</sub> which then
  goes into the kidneys)
</div>
