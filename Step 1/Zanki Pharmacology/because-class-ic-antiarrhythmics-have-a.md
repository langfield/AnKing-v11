### Text
Because <b>Class IC antiarrhythmics</b> have a <u>strong</u>
binding affinity and dissociate primarily during <u>diastole</u>,
a(n) {{c2::increased}} heart rate results in a longer {{c1::QRS}}
complex

### Extra
Increased HR shortens diastole (by shortening diastasis), thus less time for class IC to dissociate, resulting in a stronger effect on phase 0 of the action potential (and thus lengthening of QRS)
