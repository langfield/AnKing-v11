### Text
<div>
  In treating SIADH, {{c1::<b>hyper</b>}}tonic saline is given when
  treating a patient with severe symptoms, and
  {{c1::<b>iso</b>}}tonic saline is given for mild symptoms
</div>

### Extra
- Fluid restriction is the <b><i><u>first thing</u></i></b> done in
asymptomatic patients
<div><img src="paste-20422569492481.jpg"></div>
<div>
  - <b>Isotonic saline</b> is preferred in <u>mild</u> symptoms
  because it is still <i><b>relatively hypertonic</b></i> to the
  dilutional hyponatremia of SIADH (therefore <i>will still
  help</i> normalize the <u>hyponatremia</u> in a patient that is
  already fluid overloaded)
</div>
<div>
  - when a patient has <b>severe symptoms</b> (cerebral edema,
  seizures), hypertonic saline is given to <b><u>ensure</u></b>
  that the water that has entered the brain is pulled out (gives a
  <b><u>strong</u></b> osmotic impetus)
</div>
