### Text
<div>
  What effect do <b>class IB antiarrhythmics</b> have on <u>action
  potential duration</u>?
</div>
<div>
  {{c1::Shortened duration}}
</div>

### Extra
<div>
  <i>via shortening of phase 2 and 3 of the myocardial action
  potential, which decreases refractory period.</i>
</div>
<div>
  <i>This is caused by both activated and inactivated Na+ channels
  being prevented from being opened. Class IB bind and prolong the
  inactivated state, thus the QT interval is necessarily shorter by
  default (less sodium taken in, less K+ efflux needed to balance
  out charge)</i>
</div>
