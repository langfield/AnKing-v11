### Text
<div>
  One <i>class</i> of drugs that may treat <b>nephrogenic diabetes
  insipidus</b> is <b>{{c1::thiazide diuretics}}</b>, which
  enhances the uptake of NaCl and H<sub>2</sub>O in the
  <b>{{c2::proximal tubule}}</b>
</div>

### Extra
<div>
  <i><u>confusing mechanism</u>: thiazide diuretics decrease
  reabsorption of NaCl in the early DCT -> increased urinary
  excretion of Na<sup>+</sup> -> decreased ECF volume ->
  increased proximal Na<sup>+</sup> and H<sub>2</sub>O reabsorption
  -> decreased urine output</i>
</div>
