### Extra
<i>"<b>wet</b>, <b>wobbly</b>, and <b>wacky</b>"; sometimes
reversible</i>
<div>
  <div>
    <i>- urinary incontinence is caused by disruption of the
    periventricular pathways that transmit inhibitory impulses from
    the cortex to the sacral micturition center</i>
  </div>
  <div>
    <i>- apraxia is a motor disorder caused by damage to the
    <u>posterior parietal cortex</u>, where the individual has
    difficulty with <b>motor planning</b> to perform tasks /
    movements (without sensory loss or paralysis)</i>
  </div>
