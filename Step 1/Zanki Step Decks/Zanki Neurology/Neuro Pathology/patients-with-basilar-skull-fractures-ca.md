### Text
Patients with <b>basilar skull fractures</b> can have {{c1::CSF}}
leaks from the <u>nose</u> or the <u>ears</u>

### Extra
In the nose (<u>CSF Rhinorrhea)</u>, this is due to rupture of the
meningeal covering of the olfactory nerve (not a true peripheral
nerve, more of a nerve like extension of the cerebrum)

  <div>
    When CSF leaks are allowed to drip onto a sheet, may produce
    the characteristic "<b>Halo Sign</b>" of blood tinged CSF
