### Extra
<i>same side results in <u>contralateral</u> paresis because the
fibers <b>decussate</b> below the medulla; patients initially have
flaccid paralysis with hyporeflexia --> spasticity /
hyperreflexia in days to weeks due to (<u>spinal shock</u>)</i>
<div>
  <i>Specifically, the anterior portion of the cere<u>bral</u>
  peduncle - the <b>crus cerebri</b> - is being compressed
  (contains the</i> <i>motor tracts)</i>
