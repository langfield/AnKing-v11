### Text
<div>
  <b>{{c1::Epidural}} hematoma</b> is characterized by <u>rapid
  expansion</u> due to <i>high</i> arterial pressure and can
  present with a(n) <u>{{c2::scalp}} hematoma</u> as well
</div>

### Extra
As opposed to <u>subdural hematoma</u> which has a <b>slow
expansion</b> due to <u>low</u> venous pressure
<div>
  Outside of this, Scalp hematomas are typically caused by the
  birthing process (Caput succedaneum, Subgaleal hematoma - ex.
  vacuum assisted delivery / Cephalohematoma - ex. forceps assisted
  delivery)
</div>
<div>
  <i><b>Tip:</b> *C*aput *S*uccedaneum *c*rosses *s*utures- CS does
  cs, the other one doesn't- to summarize, caput succedaneum takes
  days to resolve and does cross suture lines, while
  cephalohematoma does not cross the suture lines and can take
  weeks or months to resolve.</i>
</div>
