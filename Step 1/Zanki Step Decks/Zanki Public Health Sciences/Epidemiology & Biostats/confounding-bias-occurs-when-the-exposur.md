### Text
<div>
  <b>Confounding bias</b> occurs when the exposure-disease
  relationship is muddled by the effect of an extraneous factor
  that is associated with both exposure and disease and {{c1::is
  NOT}} on the causal pathway
</div>

### Extra
<i>- Confounder appears to have an effect on crude analysis, but
upon stratification by the confounder the effect disappears</i>
<div>
  - Ex. Pulmonary disease is more common in coal workers than the
  general population (you could say being a coal worker increases
  likelihood of PD), BUT people who work in coal mines also smoke
  more frequently than the general population...
</div>
