### Text
Confounding Bias can be reduced by <b>RANDOMIZATION</b>, but other
important interventions include:
<div>
  <div>
    - <b>{{c1::Matching / Stratification}}</b> of exposure groups
  </div>
  <div>
    - <b>{{c2::crossover}}</b> studies
  </div>
  <div>
    - <b>{{c3::restriction}}</b> of characteristics
  </div>
</div>
<div>
  - multiple / repeated {{c4::<b>studies</b>}}
</div>

### Extra
<i>- Crossover studies are where subjects act as own controls</i>
<div>
  <div>
    <i>- Matching</i> <i>is where exposure groups are selected on
    the basis of important prognostic variables to eliminate a
    confounder (ex. stage of disease, age, sex) and is done in
    <u>case-control studies;</u> Stratification is analyzing them
    based on confounder</i>
  </div>
  <div>
    <i>- Restriction is limiting the range of characteristics of
    patients in the study if strong confounding characteristic is
    present</i>
  </div>
</div>
