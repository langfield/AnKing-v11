### Text
In <b>Limb Compartment Syndrome</b>, increased tissue pressure
within a <u>fascial compartment</u> of a limb <i>exceeds</i> the
perfusion pressure, leading to <b>venous</b> {{c1::<b>outflow
obstruction</b>}} and {{c2::<b>arteriolar</b>}} <b>collapse</b>

### Extra
- This leads to <b>anoxia and necrosis</b>, which results in pain
and decreased peripheral sensation
<div>
  <div>
    - irreversible tissue damage occurs between 6-8 hours after
    onset
  </div>
