Increased pressure within a fascial compartment of a limb
experiencing <u>Compartment Syndrome</u> is defined bya
<b><i>fascial compartment</i> pressure to <i>diastolic</i> pressure
gradient</b> of {{c1::< 30 mmHg}}

### Extra
- A transducer is connected to a catheter and used to measure the
intracompartmental pressure (ICP)
<div>
  - Î” pressure (diastolic blood pressure - ICP) < 30 mmHg (a
  normal ICP = 0 mmHg); as the ICP pressure rises, it gets closer
  to diastolic BP - thus a gradient < 30 means that the
  compartment pressure is super high
</div>
