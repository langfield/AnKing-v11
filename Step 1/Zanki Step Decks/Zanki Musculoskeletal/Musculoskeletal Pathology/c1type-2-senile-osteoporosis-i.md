### Text
<div>
  {{c1::<b>Type 2 ('senile')</b>}} <b>osteoporosis</b> is
  characterized by <u>normal</u> <b>osteoclast</b> activity with
  <u>decreased</u> <b>osteoblast</b> activity
</div>

### Extra
<div>
  -this is termed a “low-turnover” osteoporosis precisely because
  osteoclast activity is normal
</div>* With age, osteoblasts lose their ability to proliferate and
form bone. Osteoclast activity is not affected by the aging
process. Thus, after reaching peak bone mass around age 30,
decreased osteoblast activity with normal osteoclast activity will
result in an overall decrease in bone density(average 0.7%/year)
