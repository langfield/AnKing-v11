### Text
In nearly all patients, an <b>interscalene nerve block</b> also
causes transient ipsilateral {{c1::diaphragmatic}} paralysis

### Extra
- This is because the nerve roots of the phrenic nerve (C3-C5) pass
through the interscalene sheath
<div>
  - This block is normally done for surgical procedures involving
  shoulder / upper arm; thus do NOT do this in patients with
  chronic lung disease or contralateral phrenic nerve dysfunction
</div>
