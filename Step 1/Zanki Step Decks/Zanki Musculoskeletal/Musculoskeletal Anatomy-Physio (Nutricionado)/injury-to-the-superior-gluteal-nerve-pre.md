### Text
Injury to the <b>superior gluteal nerve</b> presents with
{{c7::trendelenburg sign}} and a gluteus medius {{c8::lurch}}

### Extra
- Trendelenburg sign is caused by the weakness of the gluteus
medius / minimus muscles causing the pelvis to sag toward the
unaffected (contralateral) side when the patient stands on the
affected leg
<div>
  - Gluteus medius lurch is seen when walking, as the patient will
  lean toward the affected (ipsilateral) side to compensate for hip
  drop
</div>
