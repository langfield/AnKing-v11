  What are the <u>three</u> main conditions which <i>precipitate
  sickling</i> in <b>sickle cell anemia</b>?
</div>
<div>
  {{c1::Hypoxemia}}, {{c2::dehydration}}, and {{c3::acidosis}}
</div>

### Extra
- including infection (acidosis)
<div>
  - these are all conditions that favor the <b>taut</b> form of
  hemoglobin (exposes a hydrophobic patch that allows for
  aggregation / polymerization)
</div>

### Sketchy
<div>
  * <b>Hypoxemia</b> decreases arterial PO<sub>2</sub>, which
  decreases the O<sub>2</sub> saturation of Hb -> increased
  deoxyhemoglobin
</div>
<div>
  * <b>Dehydration</b> causes in increase in concentration of
  deoxyhemoglobin
</div>
<div>
  * <b>Acidosis</b> shifts the oxygen dissociation curve rightward,
  causing O<sub>2</sub> release from RBCs leading to an increase in
  deoxyhemoglobin
</div>
