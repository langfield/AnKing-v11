### Text
<div>
  What <u>equation</u> may be used to calculate the <b>airflow</b>
  (Q) given the <i>pressure</i> and <i>resistance</i> of the
  airway?
</div>
<div>
  {{c1::Q = ΔP/R}}
</div>

### Extra
<div>
  <i>Q = airflow (mL/min)</i>
</div>
<div>
  <i>ΔP = pressure gradient (mmHg)
  <b>P<sub>atm</sub>-P<sub>alv</sub></b></i>
</div>
<div>
  <i>R = airway resistance (cm H<sub>2</sub>O/L/sec)</i>
</div>
<div>
  <i>**<b>note</b>: resistance <u>inversely proportional</u> to
  <b>r<sup>4</sup></b>, therefore if a vessels radius is
  <b>doubled</b>, the <b>flow</b> is <u>increased</u> by a factor
  of 16</i>
</div>
