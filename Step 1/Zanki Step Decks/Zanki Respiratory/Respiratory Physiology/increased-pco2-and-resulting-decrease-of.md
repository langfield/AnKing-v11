### Text
<div>
  Increased <b>pCO<sub>2</sub></b> and resulting decrease of
  <b>pH</b> enhancing the release of O<sub>2</sub> from hemoglobin
  is called the {{c1::Bohr}} effect
</div>

### Extra
<div>
  This occurs due to the histidine side chains on hemoglobin
  subunits
</div>
<div>
  - as tissues release CO<sub>2</sub>, RBCs convert to
  HCO<sup>3-</sup> and H<sup>+</sup>
</div>
<div>
  - HCO<sup>3-</sup> is shifted out of RBCs for Cl<sup>-</sup>,
  H<sup>+</sup> remain within the RBCs and are buffered by
  Histidine, stabilizing the deoxygenated form and decrease its
  affinity for O<sub>2</sub>
</div>
