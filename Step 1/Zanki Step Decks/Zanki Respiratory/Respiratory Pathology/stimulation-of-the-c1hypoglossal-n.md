### Text
Stimulation of the <b>{{c1::hypoglossal}} nerve</b> can be used as
a therapy for <b>obstructive sleep apnea</b> by increasing the
{{c2::diameter}} of the oropharyngeal airway

### Extra
Upper airway dilator muscles weaken during the transition from wake to sleep, leading to airway narrowing and collapse; stimulation of hypoglossal nerve causes the tongue to move forward slightly and decreases the frequency of apneic events
