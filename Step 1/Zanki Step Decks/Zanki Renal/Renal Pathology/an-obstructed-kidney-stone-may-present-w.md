### Text
<div>
  An <u>obstructed</u> <b>kidney stone</b> may present with
  <i>unilateral</i> <b>{{c1::flank}} tenderness</b> that
  <u>radiates</u> to the <b>{{c2::groin}}</b>
</div>

### Extra
<div>
  - stone must either be <b>blocking</b> any portion of the ureter
  OR the ureteropelvic / uterovesical junction for pain to form
</div>
<div>
  - pain referral depends on where the stone is trapped, localizes
  closer to the groin and away from the flank as it moves from the
  kidney through the ureters
</div>
