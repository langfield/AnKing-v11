What is the <i>next step</i> in management for a patient with
<b>hypovolemia</b> and <b>metabolic alkalosis</b> due to
<u>vomiting</u>, <u>NG tube</u> use or <u>diuretics</u>? Urine
chloride is 15 mEq/L. {{c1::IV bolus of normal saline}}

### Extra
* Saline-responsive contributors to metabolic
alkalosis (e.g. vomiting, diuretic use) generally have a low associated urine
chloride (<25 mEq/L), whereas saline-resistant metabolic alkalosis (e.g.
primary and secondary aldosteronism, Bartter syndrome, Gitelman syndrome,
exogenous alkali administration) has a high urinary chloride concentration
(>40 mEq/L) resulting from the excess mineralocorticoid effect
