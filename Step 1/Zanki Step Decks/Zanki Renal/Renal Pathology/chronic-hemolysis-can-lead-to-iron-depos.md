### Text
Chronic hemolysis can lead to iron deposition in the kidney (this process is known as {{c1::hemosiderosis}})

### Extra
<div>
  <i>- renal tubular cells pick up some of the Hb that is filtered
  into the urine and break it down into iron, which accumulates as
  hemosiderin; tubular cells eventually shed resulting in
  hemosiderinuria</i>
</div>
<div>
  - the chronic effect of this interferes with proximal tubule
  function and cause interstitial scarring and cortical infarct
</div>
