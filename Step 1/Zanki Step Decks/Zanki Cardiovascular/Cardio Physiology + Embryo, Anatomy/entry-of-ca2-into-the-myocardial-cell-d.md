  Entry of Ca<sup>2+</sup> into the myocardial cell during the
  <u>myocardial action potential</u> triggers <i>further
  release</i> of Ca<sup>2+</sup> through <b>{{c1::ryanodine}}
  receptors</b> (Ca<sup>2+</sup> release channels)
</div>

### Extra
<i>Ca<sup>2+</sup>-induced-Ca<sup>2+</sup> release; as opposed to
<b>skeletal muscle</b> where L-type calcium channels are attached
directly to ryanodine receptors (thus no dependence on
extracellular calcium influx)</i>
