<div>
  <b>Relaxation</b> of a myocardial cell occurs when:
</div>
<div>
  - Ca<sup>2+</sup> is <i>reaccumulated</i> in the sarcoplasmic
  reticulum by the action of {{c1::<u>Ca<sup>2+</sup> ATPase
  (SERCA)</u>}}
</div>
<div>
  - Ca<sup>2+</sup> is pumped out of the cell by the
  {{c2::<u>Na<sup>+</sup>/Ca<sup>2+</sup> exchanger (NCX)</u>}}
</div>

### Extra
<i>the Ca2+ ATPase (SERCA) is regulated by the protein
phospholamban. When unphosphorylated, phospholamban inhibits SERCA,
but when phosphorylated phospholamban is itself inhibited. This
leads to <b>faster</b> uptake of Ca2+ into the Sarcoplasmic
Reticulum, thereby contributing to lusitropy (the rate of
myocardial relaxation)</i>
