### Text
The <u>major determinant</u> of whether or not a coronary artery
plaque will cause ischemic myocardial injury is the <b>rate of</b>
{{c1::arterial occlusion}}

### Extra
slowly developing occlusion allows for the formation of collaterals
that could prevent myocardial necrosis; a thin fibrous cap, rich
lipid core, and active inflammation in the atheroma would all
decrease plaque stability and thus potentially promote rapid
coronary occlusion (via super imposed thrombosis) if the plaque
were to rupture
