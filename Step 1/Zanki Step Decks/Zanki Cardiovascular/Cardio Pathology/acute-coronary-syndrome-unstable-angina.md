### Text
Acute coronary syndrome (unstable angina / MI) usually occurs due
to <u>plaque rupture</u>, due to an imbalance of
<b>{{c1::metalloproteinase}}</b> activity

### Extra
ACS = unstable angina / MI; during the chronic inflammatory
progression of an atheroma, the fibrous cap is continually being
remodeled - the balance of collagen <u>synthesis</u> (by vascular
smooth muscle cells) and <u>degradation</u> (metalloproteinases
secreted by atheroma infiltrating macrophages) determines the
mechanical strength of the cap
