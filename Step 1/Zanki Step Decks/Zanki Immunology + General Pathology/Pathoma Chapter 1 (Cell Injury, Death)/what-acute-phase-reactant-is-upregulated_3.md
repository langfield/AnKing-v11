### Text
What <b>acute phase reactant</b> is <u>upregulated</u> during
inflammation to prevent iron release from macrophages and
enterocytes?
<div>
  {{c1::Hepcidin}}
</div>

### Extra
<div>
  This protein binds and inhibits the <b>Ferroportin</b> channel
  through which macrophages / enterocytes release iron to be bound
  by transferrin for transport through the blood stream
</div>
<div>
  This protein is involved in negative feedback in normal iron
  homeostasis; in hemochromatosis the negative feedback loop is
  disrupted due to inhibition of the HFE protein --> iron is
  free to leave through ferroportin
</div>
