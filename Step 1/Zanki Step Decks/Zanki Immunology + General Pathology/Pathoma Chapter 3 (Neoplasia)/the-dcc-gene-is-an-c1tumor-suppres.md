### Text
The <b>DCC</b> gene is a(n) {{c1::tumor suppresor}} that is
<u>frequently mutated</u> in <b>advanced colorectal cancer</b>

### Extra
- This gene is a <b>Dependence Receptor</b>, meaning it has two
functions when it is bound and when it is not bound (as opposed to
a classical receptor which is "on-off")
<div>
  - when DCC is bound by nectrin-1, this induces a survival signal;
  when DCC is not bound by nectrin-1, this induces an apoptotic
  signal
</div>
