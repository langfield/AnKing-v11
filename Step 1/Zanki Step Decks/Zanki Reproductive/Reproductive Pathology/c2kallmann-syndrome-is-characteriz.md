### Text
<div>
  <b>{{c2::Kallmann}} syndrome</b> is characterized by a defective
  <u>migration</u> of {{c1::GnRH}} cells and defective
  <u>formation</u> of the {{c1::olfactory bulb}}
</div>

### Extra
<div>
  In the first 10 weeks of normal embryonic development GnRH
  neurons migrate from their origin in the olfactory bulb to the
  hypothalamus
</div>
<div>
  <div>
    - Neurons originate in the olfactory placode, then pass through
    cribriform plate, along with the fibers of the olfactory
    nerves, and into the rostral forebrain, then into what becomes
    the hypothalamus
  </div>
  <div>
    - Anything that impairs the development of olfactory nerves
    prevents the progression of GnRH neurons into the hypothalamus
  </div>
  <div>
    in kallman syndrome, the olfactory bulbs are not developed
    properly most commonly due to a defect in KAL1 (ANOS1)
  </div>
  <div>
    - this encodes the Anosmin-1 protein expressed in brain,
    spinalcord, and kidney
  </div>
  <div>
    - anosmin-1 contributes to normal migration of nerve cells, and
    is a Chemoattractant for fetal olfactory epithelial cells
  </div>
  <div>
    Thus there is decreased synthesis of GnRH in the hypothalamus,
    as well as anosmia
  </div>
</div>
